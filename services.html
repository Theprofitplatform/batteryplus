Warranty Information

Our commitment to quality backed by comprehensive warranty coverage

Warranty Against Defects

Coverage as displayed on product face and top labels

Our goods are supplied with a warranty against defects for the period of time and application displayed on product face and top labels.

If our testing determines the battery is defective we will replace it, however the costs of delivering it to the warranty location and collecting it and any replacement are yours. The claim must be made within the warranty period listed on top of the battery. Dated proof of purchase is required. The warranty period for replacement starts on the date of purchase of the defective battery it replaces.

What's Not Covered

This warranty does not cover the following

A flat battery
Normal wear and tear
Physical Damage
Undercharging (sulphation)
Incorrect Application
Negligence (before or during use)
Over-charging
Spillage from over filling
Modifications to the battery
Addition of fluids other than water
Motorsport or racing activities

Your Consumer Rights

Australian Consumer Law Guarantees

This warranty is in addition to other rights and remedies available at law. Our goods come with guarantees that cannot be excluded under the Australian Consumer Law.

Your Entitlements
  • You are entitled to a replacement or refund for a major failure and for compensation for any other reasonably foreseeable loss or damage.
  • You are also entitled to have the goods repaired or replaced if the goods fail to be of acceptable quality and the failure does not amount to a major failure.

Private Use Definition

Understanding warranty coverage classifications

Private use is a vehicle used for private needs, as opposed to business uses. These vehicles are generally defined as having only four wheels and are not used to carry passengers or goods for monetary purposes.

Warranty Check Procedure

Step-by-step process when a battery is suspected faulty

1
Proof of Purchase

A dated proof of purchase must be produced.

2
Check Date & Application

Verify the purchase date and whether the battery was used in a private or commercial application.

3
Inspect for Obvious Damage

Check the battery for any visible signs of physical damage.

4
Conduct Performance Test

A performance test will be conducted. Recharging of the battery may be required to confirm test results.

Warranty Will Be Voided If

Conditions that invalidate warranty coverage

Wrong vehicle application
Beyond warranty period
Signs of mishandling
Damaged cover, casing, terminal posts, or plugs
Leaks from damaged cover or casing
Tampered codes and labels
Signs of overcharging (bulging)
Signs of undercharging (sulphation)

Important Reminders

Please keep these points in mind regarding warranty claims

A discharged/flat battery is not considered a warranty claim.

Warranty only covers manufacturing defects.

Battery may need to be returned to the manufacturer for testing if required.

If a load tester is available, it is recommended to use it to confirm the results.

Make sure to key in the correct CCA or AH, whichever is applicable on the testers.

Normal wear and tear which may mean not meeting the CCA or AH claim, is not a warranty.

Warranty period is not renewed or extended should a battery replacement be approved.
